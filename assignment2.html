My Favourite 6 Cities

Palakkad

Palakkad is one of the fourteen districts of Kerala and has no coastal line. The district opens the state to the rest of the country through the Palakkad Gap with a width of 32 to 40 Kms. Its geographical position, historical background, educational status, tourism hot-spots and above all, the development activities that are carried out, are wide and varied. The district is one of the main granaries of Kerala and its economy is primarily agricultural. The district is also the land of Palmyrahs.Palakkad is the second largest district in Kerala, after Idukki. It covers 11.53% of the state's land area and is home to 8.2% of its population. Palakkad is a major agricultural area and a major producer of paddy. It is also home to Inox Air Products, the largest producer of oxygen in Kerala. Palakkad is known as the "gateway to Kerala" because of the Palakkad Gap, a 40-kilometer mountain pass in the Western Ghats that connects Kerala to Tamil Nadu. The Anginda peak in Silent Valley National Park is the highest point in the district. The Kalpathi and Kannadi rivers flow through the city of Palakkad, and are two of the main tributaries of the Bharathapuzha. The Malampuzha dam, one of the largest dams in the district, is located about 15 kilometers from Palakkad city.

Kochi

Kochi was an important spice trading centre on the west coast of India from the 14th century onward, and maintained a trade network with Arab merchants from the pre-Islamic era. Occupied by the Portuguese in 1503, Kochi was the first of the European colonies in colonial India. It remained the main seat of Portuguese India until 1530, when Goa was chosen instead. The city was later occupied by the Dutch and the British, with the Kingdom of Cochin becoming a princely state. Kochi is a major port city in Kerala, India, known as the "Queen of the Arabian Sea". It's a blend of natural beauty and culture, and is a top tourist destination. Kochi has been a trade center since the 14th century, and was the first European colony in India when the Portuguese occupied it in 1503.The Dutch and British also occupied the city, and the Kingdom of Kochi became a princely state.Kochi is home to a variety of ethnic communities, including Jews, Konkinis, and Gujaratis. It's also known for its rich culture and traditions, including its dance forms, spices, tea, and coffee.

Kozhikode

Kozhikode, also known in English as Calicut, is a city along the Malabar Coast in the state of Kerala in India. Known as the City of Spices, Kozhikode is listed among the UNESCO's Cities of Literature. It has a corporation limit population of 609,224 and a metropolitan population of more than 2 million, making it the second most populous metropolitan area in Kerala and the 19th largest in India. Kozhikode is classified as a Tier 2 city by the Government of India. Kozhikode was the capital of the Zamorin kingdom and a major trading center for spices like cardamom and black pepper. The city's history dates back to 1042 AD, and it was the gateway to the medieval South Indian coast for traders from China, Persia, Arabia, and Europe. Vasco da Gama landed in Kozhikode in 1498, which led to the arrival of the Dutch, French, and English. Kozhikode beach is a popular destination for tourists, with many attractions including a lighthouse, piers, and a marine water aquarium. Visitors can also enjoy seafood shacks and Dolphins Point, where they can see dolphins at dawn.Kozhikode is known for its unique culture and friendly atmosphere. In 2012, it was named the "City of Sculptures" (Shilpa Nagaram) for its many architectural sculptures. Kozhikode is also home to the Sports and Education Promotion Trust (SEPT), which was established to promote sports development in India.Kozhikode is home to the Usha School of Athletics, which is run by P T Usha, an Indian track and field athlete.

Thiruvananthapuram

Thiruvananthapuram is a major tourist centre, known for the Padmanabhaswamy Temple, the beaches of Kovalam and Varkala, the backwaters of Poovar and Anchuthengu and its Western Ghats tracts of Ponmudi and the Agastya Mala. In 2012, Thiruvananthapuram was named the best Kerala city to live in, by a field survey conducted by The Times of India. In 2013, the city was ranked the fifteenth best city to live in India, in a survey conducted by India Today.Thiruvananthapuram, also known as Trivandrum, is the capital of Kerala, India. It's a major city in the south of India, known for its beaches, temples, museums, and art galleries.Thiruvananthapuram is located on the west coast of India, near the extreme south of the mainland. It's bordered by the Arabian Sea to the west, the Kollam district to the north, the Tirunelveli district to the east, and the Kanyakumari district to the south.The city became prominent in 1745 when Raja Martanda Varma made it the capital of his kingdom of Travancore.

Thrissur

Thrissur was once the capital of the Kingdom of Cochin, and was a point of contact for the Assyrians, Greeks, Persians, Arabs, Romans, Portuguese, Dutch and English. Thrissur is also known as the cultural capital of Kerala because of its cultural, spiritual and religious leanings throughout history.The city centre contains the Kerala Sangeetha Nadaka Academy, Kerala Lalithakala Akademi and Kerala Sahitya Academy.Thrissur is known as the "Land of Poorams" for its many temple festivals, including the Thrissur Pooram, where decorated elephants march to the Vadakkunnathan Temple.Thrissur is home to the Vadakkunnathan Temple, which is dedicated to Shiva and the focus of an annual festival.Thrissur is home to the Kerala Kalamandalam, Kerala Lalitakala Academy, Kerala Sangeetha Nataka Academy, and Kerala Sahithya Academy.Thrissur has been influenced by Indian, European, and Arab sources and is considered to be the oldest city on the west coast of India.Thrissur is bordered by the districts of Palakkad and Malappuram to the north, and the districts of Ernakulam and Idukki to the south.

Alapuzha

Alappuzha is a prominent tourist destination in Kerala. The town is famous for its waterways and backwaters, and has been described as the "Venice of the East" for the small canals winding through its historic centre. It plays a role as one of the primary access points for the annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race during the festival of Onam Alappuzha is also a hub for coir manufacturing, and has a thriving coir industry. Alappuzha is a district in Kerala, India known for its backwaters, beaches, and boat races. It's also known as the "Venice of the East" due to its network of waterways and canals.Alappuzha is a sandy strip of land with a long coastline, lagoons, rivers, and canals. It's the only district in Kerala without any high land or forests.Alappuzha has been a trade center since ancient times and had trade relations with ancient Greece and Rome. Some of the top attractions in Alappuzha include the backwaters, beaches, and Kuttanad, which is known as the "Rice Bowl of Kerala".
